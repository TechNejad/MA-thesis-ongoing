Towards an Ontology of Normative Role Design for LLM interactions in Multi-Agent Simulations

Mohsen Hassan Nejad

Erasmus Mundus Joint Master's Scholarship in Artificial Intelligence for Sustainable Societies (AISS) – Ongoing thesis (2025–2026)

One‑Pager Summary Conference Paper

Abstract

Ongoing master's thesis exploring how normative roles for large language models are defined and operationalized within multi‑agent simulations. As LLMs evolve into autonomous agents, their roles are encoded via system prompts, yet development and evaluation remain ad hoc and unsystematic. This project aims to develop a preliminary ontology of normative role design by mapping how roles are operationalized and how they shape outcomes.

Research Approach

  • Systematic Literature Review. Identify and analyse multi‑agent LLM simulation studies and their code to build the foundation of the ontology.
  • Five‑Layer Coding. Code each study across: (a) study context, (b) simulation setup, (c) normative design (role types, methods, ethics), (d) outcomes (behaviours, metrics) and (e) evidence (prompts, contradictions).
  • Ontology Construction. Formalise findings as knowledge triples to map role types, prompting methods, ethical framings and their relationships to outcomes.
